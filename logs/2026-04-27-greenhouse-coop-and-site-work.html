Greenhouse, Coop, and Site Work

greenhouse coop curved canopy site work fence posts field notes

Two active days on the physical build. The coop is in its new location. The curved canopy conversion is underway. The greenhouse infrastructure is taking shape.

April 27 — Coop in Place

Another fence post in the ground, and the chicken coop is moved to its new location on the site. This has been a multi-day effort — getting the ground prepped, the coop relocated, and the position set so that the surrounding work can reference it.

The chickens approve of the new space. More room, better positioning, and — once the canopy is done — better weather protection.

Picking Up Greenhouse Plastic

Picked up additional greenhouse plastic for the canopy conversion. The old flat roof on the coop is being removed and replaced with a curved canopy shape using greenhouse plastic over a bent frame. The curved profile sheds rain better than a flat roof, handles snow load differently, and gives more headroom toward the center of the structure.

Greenhouse plastic is one of those materials that does more things than its name suggests — it is UV-stabilized, passes the right wavelengths for plant growth, and is durable enough for year-round outdoor use. On the coop canopy, the light transmission is a bonus; the main goal is weather protection and a better roof shape.

April 29 — Canopy Conversion Begins

Disassembled the old flat roof. The flat design was never quite right — it held water, put awkward load on the frame, and looked like a temporary fix that had been in place too long. It came down without much regret.

Test-fit the new curved canopy shape. The bent-frame approach works — the profile looks right and the structure is solid at the attachment points. The greenhouse plastic will go over this once the framing is confirmed.

Chicken Advisory Council

The chickens were involved in this process whether we wanted them to be or not. They spent the day investigating the construction work closely — walking across the materials, inspecting the framing from underneath, and generally being in the way. It is their house, so they get a vote. Their feedback was inconclusive but enthusiastic.

Site State After These Two Days

  • Coop: moved to new location, stable
  • Canopy: old flat roof removed, curved frame test-fit complete
  • Greenhouse plastic: sourced and on site, ready for canopy installation
  • Fence posts: continuing to go in as ground work proceeds
  • Solar: 700W active harvest running, battery bank topped up
  • Tool charging: running from solar, operational
Field note: The curved canopy is one of those changes that is obviously right once you commit to it. The flat roof was a compromise from the start. The curved profile is how greenhouse structures are supposed to work — it sheds water, distributes load, and gives the structure better geometry at every point.
