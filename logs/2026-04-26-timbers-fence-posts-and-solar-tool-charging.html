Timbers, Fence Posts, and Solar-Charged Tools

site work fence posts solar tool charging swale field notes

The clouds broke today. Decided to take advantage of it and get outside. One of those good days where a lot of ground-level work happened at once.

What Got Done

Today's work was pure site prep — the kind of physical groundwork that does not look like much in a photo but makes everything easier later.

Cut timbers to length for the ongoing build. Getting those squared away and ready means less scrambling when they are needed mid-project and the weather is not cooperating.

Got the fence posts set. The ground here took some persuading, but they are in and solid. The posts establish the lines that the rest of the site work will reference, so getting them right matters.

Swale Excavation: Runoff Control Near the Container

Started the swale excavation near the steel container. This is drainage work — cutting a shallow channel in the right place so that surface runoff moves away from the container and toward somewhere it can spread out and soak in rather than pooling against the structure.

The container sits in a spot that collects water during heavy rain. A swale is the low-tech fix: a few hours of digging in the right spot, shaped correctly, and the water problem mostly solves itself. There is more to do here, but the start is in the ground.

One More Find

Repurposed the screen door from the house for the build. Salvaging useful material from one part of the property for another is just common sense — no point in buying something new when a working version is already on site and not being used where it is.

Tool Charging from Solar — First Field Use

The sun stayed out long enough to get a full charge on the cordless tool batteries. This is the first time on this build that the solar array has been used for practical field work — not just charging the battery bank, but directly powering the tools being used on site.

That is the point of the whole power setup: not to prove that a solar panel can blink a light, but to make the harvest useful for real work. Today it was tool batteries. That is a start.

Chickens Consulted

Also had a conversation with the chickens about moving into their new space. They had mixed feelings. The new area is larger and better positioned. They will come around.

Field note: Full sun days are the window for ground work. When the weather cooperates, cover as much ground as possible. The swale, the posts, the timbers, and the tool charge all happened in the same day because the conditions were right.
