Springhouse Build Log Reborn

build log off-grid site work field notes practical resilience

The Springhouse build log is active again. This is the record of a working rural property build — the kind where the physical work comes first and the plans follow.

Springhouse is not a lifestyle concept or a finished product. It is a property being built, one step at a time, with a clear focus on making it more capable and more resilient. The record here covers what actually gets done: solar panels going up, greenhouse framing going in, coop systems being moved and improved, site drainage being cut, tools charging off harvested power, and the steady decisions that add up to a place that works.

This log exists because good build records are useful. They are useful to the person doing the work — when a problem comes back around, you want a record of what you tried before. They are useful to anyone else following along, because concrete details about what worked and what failed are more valuable than general advice about how things should go in theory.

What This Build Covers

The physical side of Springhouse is organized around a few core areas:

  • Solar power and DC distribution. The property runs on a combination of 24V and 12V DC systems fed by solar panels and a lead-acid battery bank. The goal is to run real loads — tools, monitoring equipment, greenhouse and coop support — directly on DC without unnecessary conversion losses.
  • Greenhouse construction. A working greenhouse module is being built out: framing, canopy, ventilation, and growing infrastructure. This is a field build, not a kit.
  • Coop and animal systems. The chicken coop has been relocated and is being improved. Curved canopy, better layout, and a proper position on the site.
  • Site work. Timbers, fence posts, swale excavation, drainage, access, and the kind of ground-level work that makes a property functional before anything more refined can happen.
  • Field notes. What worked, what failed, what needs to change — recorded as it happens.

The Parts in Motion

One of the things that makes this build what it is: a lot of the components being used are parts collected over years of tinkering and hands-on work. Components from old projects, boards from community spaces, hardware that has been moved from box to project to shelf and back again. Putting them into a working system instead of leaving them in storage is part of the point.

That includes a Raspberry Pi 3 that was gifted at a makerspace years ago. It is still running and still useful, which is about as good as a component can be.

The physical build is the easier part of this project. Getting all the pieces actually assembled and working together in real outdoor conditions — that is where the real decisions get made.

Why This Log Matters

The log moves forward from here with regular, focused entries — what changed, what worked, what failed, and what comes next. A running account of how the site actually comes together, recorded as it happens.

Good build notes are worth keeping. They are useful now and more useful later.
